I'm pretty proud to announce that I'll be speaking at the new open source, polyglot conference for application developers and maintainers DevNation 2014. It will be co-located with Red Hat Summit and will offer sessions, labs, and panels geared for those who build with open source.

 As of today the <a href="http://www.devnation.org/#cfp" target="_blank">official call for papers</a> starts. Now it's on you to join the <a href="http://www.devnation.org/#speakers" target="_blank">early announced speakers</a> among myself, Mike Milinkovich (Eclipse), Chris Aniszczyk (Twitter), Arun Gupta (Red Hat).

<br>
 While there will not be formal tracks you can send in proposals among the following topics that will likely fit into overall vibe including:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
 <li>Enterprise Application Development (e.g. concurrent, multi-user applications with data stores, security, transactions, etc)</li>
 <li>Front-end Development (e.g. display technologies, client-side scripting and layout, templating engines)</li>
 <li>Mobile Development (e.g. Android, iOS, Cordova, HTML5 for mobile)</li>
 <li>Big Data (e.g. RDBMS and NoSQL, persistent storage and associated computational problems with large data stores)</li>
 <li>Application Integration (Service-Oriented Architecture)</li>
 <li>DevOps and Continuous Delivery (e.g. build and deployment environments, administration and management)</li>
 <li>Performance and Tuning (e.g. Analysis and techniques to get the best out of your applications)</li>
 <li>Platform Development (ie. C++ and *nix applications)</li>
 <li>Cool Stuff (Any helpful bits you'd love to share with your peers)</li>
</ul>
<br>
 You can hook up with this awesome new conference by joining the twitter discussion <a href="http://twitter.com/DevNationConf" target="_blank">@DevNationConf</a>&nbsp;or tweet with the official&nbsp;#DevNation14 tag and don't forget to check back with the <a href="" target="_blank">conference website</a> frequently for new announcements.
